Taxonomy:

The Pomacentridae family members, which has over 400 different types of small, vividly coloured reef fish, comprises clownfish. They are generally categorised within the Amphiprion genus, that includes more than Thirty species that are known.

Discovery:

In the 18th and 19th centuries, Western scholars made the first written accounts of clownfish during trips to the Indo-Pacific area region. But since these fish are vital to the community's ecology and cultural backgrounds, it's probable indigenous inhabitants of these areas have been aware concerning them for quite some time.
